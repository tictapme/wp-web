In recent years, there is more and more talk about the Internet of Things (IoT). More and more IoT-based solutions are being adopted in sectors such as industry, railway, retail, etc. However, despite the many advantages of IoT, we must also consider its drawbacks to determine if it is the best solution for our business.<\/p>\n

TicTAP is not and is not based on IoT. Still, TicTAP has some aspects in common, while in others it overcomes some limitations of the Internet of Things.<\/p>\n

Does this mean that TicTAP is better than IoT or vice versa? Not at all. The most honest and realistic answer possible is: “it depends on your case and the needs of your business.”<\/p>\n

That is why in this article we are going to see the advantages and disadvantages or limitations of IoT and TicTAP so that you can assess which solution best suits your requirements.<\/p>\n\t\t\t\t\t<\/div>\n\t\t\t\t\t\t<\/div>\n\t\t\t\t<\/div>\n\t\t\t\t

What is the Internet of Things (IoT)?<\/h2>\t\t<\/div>\n\t\t\t\t<\/div>\n\t\t\t\t
\n\t\t\t\t\t\t

The IoT consists of the virtual connection between things, processes, people, animals and any real thing or object that we have in our world.<\/p>\n

That is, with IoT, any physical object in our environment is capable of automatically communicating with any other physical object without human intervention or human-machine interaction.<\/p>\n\t\t\t\t\t<\/div>\n\t\t\t\t\t\t<\/div>\n\t\t\t\t<\/div>\n\t\t\t\t\t\t<\/div>\n\t\t\t\t\t<\/div>\n\t\t<\/div>\n\t\t\t\t\t\t\t\t<\/div>\n\t\t\t\t\t<\/div>\n\t\t<\/section>\n\t\t\t\t

\n\t\t\t\t
\n\t\t\t\t\t\t\t\t
\n\t\t\t\t

With Internet of Things, any object can measure and report data. This data can range from simple numbers collected by a sensor, to much more complex discoveries made by devices that measure and analyze multiple data streams at the same time.<\/p>\n

In a factory plant, the IoT can be used so that the different machines can quantify and report the operation and performance of the plant, warn about breakdowns or dangerous parameters (excess temperature, etc.).<\/p>\n\t\t\t\t\t<\/div>\n\t\t\t\t\t\t<\/div>\n\t\t\t\t<\/div>\n\t\t\t\t

Advantages of Internet of Things (IoT)<\/h3>\n

The main advantages of the Internet of Things are:<\/p>\n

    \n
  1. Predictive and remote maintenance: <\/strong> IoT devices allow collecting information on their status in real time and sending it to the cloud to be monitored remotely. This information can be used to perform predictive maintenance of the equipment or facilities on which the data was sent.<\/li>\n
  2. Communication for automation and control: <\/strong> IoT enables communication between devices, also known as Machine-to-Machine (M2M) communication.
    \nThis direct communication between physical devices without human intervention allows the different devices to work connected to each other, in a transparent way and with less inefficiencies as well as better quality.
    \nFurthermore, as they are connected to the network, they allow them to be controlled digitally and centrally with a wireless infrastructure.<\/li>\n
  3. Saving time and money: <\/strong> Direct communication between machines without human intervention is faster, and it also means that human resources are free from certain tasks since the machines are already synchronized with each other . This frees up time for people who can spend on other tasks, or the number of people needed can be reduced.<\/li>\n
  4. Better decisions: <\/strong> IoT allows you to collect more information, which will always help you to make decisions based on real data. Thus, decisions based on them are likely to be much more realistic and accurate than those based on a more superficial monitoring of the situation or even estimates.<\/li>\n
  5. Easier monitoring: <\/strong> IoT devices can create reports based on the capture of information in real time. In this way the monitoring of the activity or the status of assets, stock, etc. it is much easier and real.<\/li>\n
  6. Smart logistics: <\/strong> One of the biggest difficulties in logistics is getting processes to be well coordinated. IoT devices can send data immediately and in real time. For example, in a transport company, vehicles can be monitored with the help of devices connected via GPS and software. This allows remote management of operations, real-time information on variations and unforeseen events, and therefore a quick response and good coordination.<\/li>\n
  7. Increased industrial security: <\/strong> IoT devices allow monitoring values \u200b\u200bthat may be critical for plant safety, such as temperature or pressure in certain areas, machines or facilities. In this way they allow you to avoid claims.<\/li>\n
  8. Increased occupational safety: <\/strong> Apart from avoiding damage to facilities and their machines, IoT devices are of great help to take care of the physical integrity of your operators and workers. For example, a wearable object can collect biometric data from the user, such as the heart rate. These data allow us to know the status of the worker and determine how to improve working conditions if possible.<\/li>\n
  9. Make your company more environmentally friendly: <\/strong> IoT devices can allow you to obtain information on energy and resource expenditure. They can do it on a single machine or on the entire plant or factory. Information such as the consumption of electricity, water and other resources can be of great value to detect anomalies, excesses, etc. and be able to find a solution. For example, an intelligent machine can use the collected data to enter a state of lower consumption when possible, thus minimizing electricity consumption.<\/li>\n<\/ol>\n\t\t\t\t\t<\/div>\n\t\t\t\t\t\t<\/div>\n\t\t\t\t<\/div>\n\t\t\t\t

    Disadvantages of Internet of Things<\/h3>\n

    The main advantages are:<\/p>\n

      \n
    1. The information is not encrypted <\/strong>, so it does not offer total security.
      \nIncompatibilities: IoT is not yet standardized. This leads to compatibility problems in communication and interaction between machines and IoT devices.<\/li>\n
    2. The software is not totally secure and can be hacked. <\/strong><\/li>\n
    3. Expensive connection or with limitations such as movement: <\/strong> If we are talking about IoT devices that work in the same factory, we will not have this problem.
      \nBut if you are looking for a solution to be able to track assets, parts or products that can even cross different countries, you cannot count on them always having a connection within reach.
      \nIn addition, the device will need to be able to connect via GPS or to the mobile network with its corresponding roaming consequences. All of this makes each IoT device and necessary infrastructure significantly more expensive in situations like this.<\/li>\n<\/ol>\n\t\t\t\t\t<\/div>\n\t\t\t\t\t\t<\/div>\n\t\t\t\t<\/div>\n\t\t\t\t

        Limitations of TicTAP<\/h3>\n

        We like to be honest and that is why we believe that this information is basic so that you can make the best decision for your company.<\/p>\n

        The main difference between TicTAP and IoT technology is that TicTAP does not automate or eliminate human intervention. <\/strong> As we have commented in the previous section, TicTAP ensures that maintenance, access to user manuals of the products, registration in the traceability, update of the stock, etc. be faster, more effective and therefore cheaper. But an operator or user is required to bring their mobile closer to the label to create the data record.<\/p>\n

        Another differentiating aspect is that TicTAP is not an actively generating data system. If you require a system that captures and sends certain parameters in real time, an IoT-based solution or another alternative will be more convenient. <\/strong><\/p>\n

        Finally, TicTAP is not intended to centralize the operation of facilities or devices. <\/strong> If you need to be able to activate or stop, manually or according to a schedule, the devices or machines in one or more buildings, a factory , etc., TicTAP will not be the solution you are looking for either.<\/p>\n\t\t\t\t\t<\/div>\n\t\t\t\t\t\t<\/div>\n\t\t\t\t<\/div>\n\t\t\t\t
